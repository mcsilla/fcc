Ilona Zrínyi

One of the greatest hungarian heroines, patriots and fighters for freedom.

Major events in Ilona's life

  • 1643. Ilona was born in Ozalj, Croatia. From her childhood she was known for her beauty and good education.
  • 1666. She married Francis Rákóczi, with whom she had three children.
  • 1676. Her husband died. The widowed Ilona requested guardianship of her children and was granted it, against the advice of Emperor Leopold I's advisers and against Francis I's will. In this way she also retained control over the vast Rákóczi estates
  • 1682. She married Imre Thököly and became an active partner in her second husband's Kuruc uprising against the Habsburgs.
  • 1683. After their defeat at the Battle of Vienna, both the Ottoman forces and Thököly's allied kuruc fighters had no choice but to retreat, and Thököly quickly lost one Rákóczi castle after another.
  • 1685. The Imperial army surrounded the last remaining stronghold, Palanok Castle in Munkács. Ilona Zrínyi alone defended the castle for three years.
  • 1688. Ilona had no choice but to surrender the castle, with the understanding that the defenders would receive amnesty from the Emperor, and that the Rákóczi estates would remain in her children's name. Under this agreement, she and her children traveled immediately to Vienna, where in violation of the pact the children were taken from her. Ilona lived until 1691 in the convent of the Ursulines, where her daughter Julianna was also raised. Her son Francis was immediately taken to the Jesuit school in Neuhaus.
  • 1691. When Habsburg General Heisler was captured by Thököly, a prisoner exchange was arranged, and Ilona joined her husband in Transylvania.
  • 1699. After the Treaty of Karlowitz was signed, both spouses had to go into exile in the Ottoman Empire. The countess lived in Galata, district of Constantinople, and later in Izmit.
  • 1703. She died in Izmit. She was buried in the French church of Saint Benoit in Galata.
